Philips Lumea Prestige PL5137, corded variant, two attachments (body and face). IPL not laser, so the energy spectrum is broader and the per-flash dose is lower than a clinic device. The trade-off is more sessions for less money: at home, every two weeks for the first eight weeks, then monthly maintenance.

Verdict pending. The published Lumea data shows ~70% hair reduction at 12 weeks for users who match the device's skin-tone window (Fitzpatrick I to IV) on dark hair. Reviewing this in 12 weeks against my own baseline rather than reading off the marketing.

## Setup

Plug-in (not battery), so the flash count per session is unlimited. Skin-tone sensor gates the device on each flash, refusing to fire on tones outside the safe range. The face attachment has a smaller window for around the cheekbones and upper lip. Eyes get covered every session regardless of what the marketing says.
