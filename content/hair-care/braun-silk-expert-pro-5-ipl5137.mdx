Braun's Silk·Expert Pro 5, model IPL5137, the corded at-home IPL with the SensoAdapt skin-tone reader on the head and two attachments (body + face). IPL not laser, so the energy spectrum is broader and the per-flash dose is lower than a clinic device. The trade-off is more sessions for less money: at home, every two weeks for the first eight weeks, then monthly maintenance.

Verdict pending. Braun's published clinical data shows up to 80% hair reduction at 12 weeks on the body and around 70% on the face for users in the device's skin-tone window (Fitzpatrick I to IV) with dark hair. Reviewing this in 12 weeks against my own baseline rather than reading off the marketing.

## Setup

Plug-in (not battery), so the flash count per session is unlimited. SensoAdapt reads skin tone before each flash and gates the device, refusing to fire on tones outside the safe range. The face attachment has a smaller window for around the cheekbones and upper lip; the body attachment is larger and faster for legs and arms. Eyes get covered every session regardless of what the marketing says.

## Why Braun over Philips Lumea

I cross-shopped both. The Lumea Prestige and the Silk·Expert Pro 5 are roughly equivalent on flash count, gentle/normal modes, and skin-tone sensing. Braun won on three small things: faster auto-flash mode (twice as fast at the highest setting), a slightly lower per-session price after the launch discount, and the 100-day money-back if it does not deliver. The 5-year warranty is a tie-breaker if you trust IPL to last that long.
