Prescription-strength antifungal shampoo, ketoconazole at 2% (the OTC Nizoral A-D bottles are the 1% version). Padagis is the generic manufacturer my US pharmacy fills against the Rx, packaged in a plain 120 ml red bottle. Same active and concentration as the brand-name Nizoral 2% prescription, half the cash price after the pharmacy's GoodRx-style discount.

Twice a week in the shower: lather on dry-feeling scalp, leave on for the five minutes the prescribing literature says to (long enough to brush teeth and shave), rinse, then run the regular shampoo and conditioner sequence after so the scalp does not feel stripped. The off-days I use the Olaplex bond-maintenance shampoo on its own.

This is genuinely medicated, not a marketing claim. If a regular anti-dandruff shampoo (zinc pyrithione or salicylic acid) is not holding the flake/itch back, the 2% ketoconazole is the next escalation a dermatologist tends to hand out.
