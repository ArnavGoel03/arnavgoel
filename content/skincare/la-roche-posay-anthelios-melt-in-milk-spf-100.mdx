The Anthelios Melt-In Milk in SPF 100 is the body-and-face sunscreen I keep coming back to for high-UV days, beach days, and anywhere I am out for over an hour. SPF 100 is overkill on paper (the difference between 50 and 100 is 98% vs 99% UVB blocked), but in practice the higher number keeps real-world coverage inside a useful window even when application is sloppy.

The "milk" texture is the actual selling point. It absorbs without the white cast or pasty drag that most American chemical sunscreens above SPF 50 have. La Roche-Posay's antioxidant complex (Cell-Ox Shield) is the thing the European Anthelios formula is famous for, and the US version finally got it.

## Routine

A nickel-sized amount on the face, two more on the chest and arms before any long day outside. Sub for the K-beauty SPFs I rotate when I am only inside or commuting. Subscribe & Save runs every 5 months, which lines up with how fast I go through a 5 fl oz tube in San Diego summers.

## Notes

Oxybenzone-free, fragrance-free, the Anthelios line was the first US-side LRP launch to drop oxybenzone before the Hawaii reef ban took effect. If you have sensitive skin around the eyes this is one of the few SPF 100 formulas that does not sting.
