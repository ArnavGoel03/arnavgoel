## Why I bought it

After a six-month run with a high-end cleanser that was leaving my cheeks tight by the third wash of the week, I wanted something boring. CeraVe Hydrating was the most frequently recommended option across r/skincareaddiction and from the two dermatologists I'd asked, so I picked up the 12oz pump.

## How I use it

Once a day, in the evening, as the second cleanse after an oil-based first cleanse. Occasionally in the morning if my skin feels especially dry, but usually I just rinse with water then.

## What it does well

The pH is in the right range (around 5.5), so it doesn't leave my barrier feeling compromised the way higher-pH foaming cleansers do. The ceramide + hyaluronic acid combo means even if I'm lazy about moisturizing afterward, I'm not waking up with flaky patches.

## What it doesn't do

It won't deeply clean a heavy SPF layer on its own — that's what the first cleanse is for. And if you like your cleanser to feel like a *ritual*, this isn't it. It's a tool.

## Verdict

The price-to-performance ratio is absurd. I keep a backup in my closet.
