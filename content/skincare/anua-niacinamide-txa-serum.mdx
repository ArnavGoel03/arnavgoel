## Why I bought it

After writing off The Ordinary's niacinamide for pilling under every moisturizer I own, I wanted another shot at the active without the chemistry-experiment texture. The tranexamic acid was the hook — TXA is one of the better-supported ingredients for post-inflammatory marks, and getting both in one bottle at this price felt worth trying.

## How I use it

Morning and evening, two to three drops, after toner and before moisturizer. I focus on the sides of my nose and the jawline where I still carry marks from a bad breakout last fall.

## What it does well

The texture is the real upgrade. It's watery-light, absorbs in about thirty seconds, and doesn't leave a tacky film — which means every moisturizer I've layered over it has cooperated. That alone solves my biggest complaint from the Ordinary era.

On the efficacy side, six weeks in, a couple of old marks along my jaw are noticeably lighter. Not erased, but fading. That's about what I'd expect from TXA at a reasonable concentration.

## What it doesn't do

It won't touch deep PIH on a short timeline, and it's not a replacement for sunscreen or exfoliation — it's a supporting active. If you're expecting a before-and-after in two weeks, this isn't that.

## Verdict

This is what I wanted the Ordinary niacinamide to be. Staying in the rotation.
