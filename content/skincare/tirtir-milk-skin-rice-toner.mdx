## Why this one

Most "hydrating toners" are basically glycerin and water, they feel hydrating in the moment and evaporate by lunch. The rice bran + niacinamide + ceramide stack here actually leaves my barrier feeling settled hours later. That's the difference I was looking for.

## Routine

Two passes after cleansing, before serum. On dry days I'll layer it three or four times, pat in, wait thirty seconds, pat in again, which is functionally a hydration mask.

## Texture

Genuinely milky, not white-cream. It absorbs in seconds and leaves no residue. People who expect a clear, watery toner sometimes get thrown by it; once you understand it's a thin essence-toner hybrid it makes sense.

## What it doesn't do

Doesn't exfoliate, doesn't brighten dramatically, doesn't replace a moisturizer. It's a foundational hydration step.
