> **This is a prescription drug, not a skincare product.** Isotroin-20 is Cipla's brand of oral isotretinoin (the same molecule as Roaccutane / Accutane). It is dispensed by prescription only, taken under a dermatologist's supervision, and is on this site only because it sat in my acne-treatment timeline. It does not belong in anyone else's "shelf" without an actual prescription, baseline blood work, and ongoing monitoring.

## What it is

Oral isotretinoin, 20mg per softgel capsule. The course length, dose, and stop date are decided by the prescribing dermatologist based on weight, severity, and how the skin and labs respond, not by anything I write here. The 15-capsule strip from this order was a partial month of a longer course; I am not publishing the schedule because the right schedule is the one a derm sets for the person taking it.

## Why this is on a skincare site

Two reasons. One, it is honest, oral isotretinoin is the single most effective tool for nodulocystic and treatment-resistant acne, and pretending it does not exist on a "what worked for my skin" log would be dishonest. Two, the post-isotretinoin skin barrier behaves differently for months afterward, which directly affects which moisturisers, sunscreens, and actives I rotate into the routine. The reviews of those products read more honestly with this context attached.

## Real, non-negotiable cons

These are not "minor side effects." They are the reason this drug requires a derm.

- **Dryness everywhere**, lips crack, nostrils bleed, eyes feel sandy, scalp flakes. Lip balm becomes a five-times-a-day habit.
- **Sun sensitivity** spikes hard. SPF moves from "good idea" to "non-negotiable."
- **Mood effects** are real for some people. Any change in mood, sleep, or motivation needs to be flagged to the prescribing derm immediately, not next visit.
- **Liver enzymes and lipids** can move; your derm will pull blood work before and during the course.
- **Teratogenic.** Pregnancy on isotretinoin causes severe birth defects. Anyone who can become pregnant goes through a separate pregnancy-prevention protocol; this is not optional.
- It is **not a supplement**, do not stack it with vitamin A supplements, do not "try" it from someone else's strip.
