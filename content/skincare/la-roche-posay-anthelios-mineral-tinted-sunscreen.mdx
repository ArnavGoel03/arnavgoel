## Why a tinted mineral

Mineral (physical) sunscreens are the right call when your skin is irritated, post-procedure, or just sensitive — fewer chances to react than with chemical filters. The trade-off is the cosmetic one: most mineral SPFs leave a chalky, ghostly cast. The iron oxide tint in this one fixes that.

## How I use it

Two finger-lengths every morning, after moisturizer, before any base makeup. Reapplied with a small mist + tap-down at lunch on outdoor days.

## What it does well

The universal tint blends across a wide range of skin tones — not perfect on the deepest or palest, but workable. Finish is genuinely matte; my T-zone doesn't break through it the way it does with dewier sunscreens. SPF protection has held up on multi-hour outdoor days.

## What it doesn't do

The bottle is 50 ml. At an SPF-appropriate two-finger dose every day, you're reordering every two months. Per-use it's fine; per-bottle it stings.

## Verdict

The mineral SPF I trust enough to repurchase. If chemical filters give your skin trouble, this is the one.
