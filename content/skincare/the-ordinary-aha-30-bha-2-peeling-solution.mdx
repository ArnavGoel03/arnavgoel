The Ordinary's notorious red mask, 30% AHAs (glycolic, lactic, tartaric, citric) on top of 2% salicylic. Strong enough that the bottle warns you to keep the dwell under ten minutes; the colour is the brand's idea of a hazard sign. Used once a month at the most.

Slotted into the same monthly cadence as a dermaplaning pass, with a fifteen-day offset. Practically: dermaplane on day 0, peeling solution on day 15, dermaplane again on day 30, peel on day 45, and so on. The two are doing different work — the dermaplane lifts vellus hair and the very top layer of dead skin physically, the peel goes deeper chemically — so alternating them at this spacing avoids stacking irritation while keeping the cell turnover steady.

Wash off, neutralise with cool water, no actives the same evening, and reinforce with a barrier moisturiser the next morning. Verdict on long-run texture and tone, plus whether the once-a-month rhythm is enough or needs to step up to every three weeks, pending real notes.
