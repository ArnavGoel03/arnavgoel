## Why I bought it

I needed a heavier moisturizer for dry-skin weeks, the kind of product that actually fixes parched cheeks overnight, not the lightweight gel I use the rest of the year. Lipikar shows up everywhere people recommend "dermatologist-grade" moisturizers, and the price-to-volume ratio was hard to argue with.

## How I use it

Nightly in dry weeks, layered after a serum or as the only step on truly cracked-feeling skin days. On my body, elbows, knees, anywhere that flakes, daily after a shower. The same bottle handles both.

## What it does well

It rebuilds. Within two or three nights, dry patches I'd been chasing for a week with lighter products are gone. The shea butter and niacinamide combo is doing real work, barrier feel improves audibly.

The fragrance-free formula matters. A lot of "sensitive" moisturizers still pack perfume that wrecks compromised skin. This one doesn't.

## What it doesn't do

In summer or for combination/oily skin, this is too much. I switch to a lighter moisturizer when humidity is up. Using Lipikar in July would mean a slick face by 10 a.m.
