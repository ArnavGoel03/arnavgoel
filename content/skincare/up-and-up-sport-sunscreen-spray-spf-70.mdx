Target's house-brand chemical sunscreen aerosol, SPF 70, the standard avobenzone-homosalate-octisalate-octocrylene blend most US drugstore sprays use. Returned the same day, bought it as a backup for a beach trip and the spray cone was too narrow and too inconsistent to actually cover skin.

Spray sunscreens are a category problem more than a brand problem — the FDA's actual usage rate guidance assumes the user re-coats every two hours and most sprays do not deposit enough active to hit the labeled SPF in real conditions. Going back to physical sunscreens (Anthelios, Beauty of Joseon) for actual sun exposure; this stays off the shelf.
