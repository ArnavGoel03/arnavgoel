## Why this one over the original

The classic orange Clean It Zero is a fine cleansing balm. The green "Pore Clarifying" variant adds AHA, PHA, and tea tree — actual functional ingredients targeting blackheads and congestion. If your skin runs oily or you get clogged pores along your nose and chin, the green is the meaningful upgrade.

## Routine

First cleanse in the evening. Scoop a fingertip's worth onto a dry face, massage for thirty to sixty seconds, emulsify with water, rinse. Follow with a gentle water-based cleanser as the second step.

## What it does well

The texture is exactly right — solid enough to scoop cleanly, oils up immediately on contact with skin, emulsifies to a milky consistency without going chalky. SPF and full-coverage makeup come off without scrubbing, which matters more than people think for not aggravating active breakouts.

## When to skip it

Sensitive-skin weeks, post-procedure recovery, or if you're already running another exfoliant in your routine — the AHAs add up faster than they look like they should. On those weeks I switch back to a non-exfoliating balm.

## Verdict

Specialty cleansing balm for a specific need, not a general daily. Earns its rotation.
