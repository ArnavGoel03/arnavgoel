## What these tools actually do

The honest list is short:
- Pre-chilled in the fridge, both tools reduce morning puffiness — this is just cold therapy, but it works.
- Slow gua sha strokes feel good and may move lymph fluid mildly. The "jaw sculpting" claims are not supported by anything serious.
- Forces you to slow down and apply serums or oils more deliberately, which is its own benefit.

## What they don't do

They don't lift your face, change your bone structure, or replace skincare. If a tool promises permanent contouring, the tool is lying.

## Routine

Roller in the morning over a hydrating serum, three or four passes per area while my face wakes up. Gua sha once or twice a week in the evening with a facial oil, for relaxation more than results.

## On material

A real jade tool at this price would be unusual. Most "green jade" rollers and gua shas at the $10 mark are dyed quartz or aventurine. That's fine for the cold-therapy use case — the material doesn't matter for that — but call it what it is.

## Verdict

Worth the money as a low-stakes intro. If you want a serious gua sha practice, look up a specific stone (real jade, rose quartz, bian) from a tool maker who tells you the source.
