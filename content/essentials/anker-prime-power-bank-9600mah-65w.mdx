The Anker Prime Power Bank, 9,600 mAh, with the killer feature: foldable AC prongs on the back. It is a power bank when you carry it, and a 65W wall charger when you plug it into a socket. One device replaces two in the bag.

The 65W output through a single USB-C port is enough to fast-charge the M-series MacBook Air at full speed, top up an iPhone or iPad in 30 minutes, and power a Galaxy at PPS speeds. Two USB-C ports mean a phone and a laptop can charge simultaneously without unplugging.

## What it does well

The little OLED on the front is the kind of small thing that becomes the default once you have it. It shows the current pack-charge percentage, which port is delivering what wattage, and an estimated time-to-full when plugged into the wall. No more guessing whether the bank is full enough to leave on a flight.

The body is the size of a thick deck of cards. Heavier than the older Anker 535-class banks, but the integrated wall plug is what you are paying for in mass.

## Caveats

9,600 mAh is enough for a full iPhone charge cycle plus a top-up, or about half a 13" MacBook Air. Not a big enough reservoir for an all-day untethered laptop session — pair it with a wall socket once a day. The 65W ceiling drops to 45W on USB-C 1 and 20W on USB-C 2 when both ports are pulling at once, which is fine for phone + laptop but not for two laptops.

## Vs. the older Anker 735 Nano II charger

The Nano II is a wall charger, full stop — no battery. The Prime is the same form factor + footprint as a small power bank with the wall plug grafted on, which is why it sits in essentials and the Nano II moved to miscellaneous. Different jobs.
