DualSense, the PS5 controller. Sometimes called the DualShock 5 even though Sony retired the DualShock name with the PS4 generation — the haptic and adaptive-trigger system was different enough to earn a new line.

Two things make it the best gamepad shipping right now: the haptic motors are voice-coil, not eccentric-rotating, so the rumble has actual texture (you can feel rain hit a windshield differently from gravel underfoot in Astro Bot or Returnal). And the L2/R2 triggers can apply variable resistance under the finger — a bowstring drawing back, a brake locking up, the tension in a slingshot. Games that lean on these read as tactile in a way no other controller does.

## Trade-offs

Battery life is shorter than the DualShock 4 — about 8-10 hours of continuous play before needing a charge. The pull on the haptics is what eats it. I keep a USB-C cable plugged into the back of the console so it tops up between sessions without thinking about it.

The stick drift problem that hit the DualShock 4 hard appears to be slower on the DualSense, but it is still a hardware reality of mass-market analog sticks. Two and a half years in, mine is fine. Spare controllers get bought when, not if.
