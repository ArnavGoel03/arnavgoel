The original-form-factor PlayStation 5 with the disc drive — not the Slim, not the Pro. The taller white tower with the chunky split-side casing and the proper 4K UHD Blu-ray drive on the bottom. Mine is the 2022 hardware revision (CFI-1200 series), the lighter, slightly more efficient board inside the same chassis Sony shipped at launch.

The disc edition is the only one I considered. The Digital matched my actual usage (everything is downloaded anyway) but the disc drive is what makes the console resellable later — and what makes it the host for the PSVR2 headset that lives next to it.

## Why the original, not the Slim

The Slim shipped later with a smaller footprint and a detachable disc drive sold separately. Same chip, same performance. If I were buying today I would buy the Slim. I bought the original because that is what was available the week I picked one up, and there is no reason to swap.

## What it does well

DualSense controller is still the best gamepad on the market — the haptic resolution and the adaptive triggers are not gimmicks, they read as a real signal in the games that use them well. Quick Resume across multiple titles is genuinely fast. The fan note is barely audible at moderate load.

## What it does not do well

The base 825 GB drive fills up in a hurry once you install three or four current-gen games. Plan on adding an internal NVMe (Gen4, with heatsink) within the first six months. The boot time after a full system update remains slower than the equivalent Xbox.
