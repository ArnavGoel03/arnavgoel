Apple's 15-inch MacBook Air on the M4, base config with 16 GB unified memory and 256 GB storage. Daily driver. Fanless, 18 hours of real-world battery on writing-and-browsing days, the same Liquid Retina display the M2 generation popularized at this size. The one practical jump from the M3 is that 16 GB is now the floor instead of 8 GB.

## The lineage

- **M1 MacBook Air**, won at a WhiteHat Jr hackathon. First Apple Silicon machine, the one that proved fanless ARM was actually viable for full dev work.
- **M2 MacBook Air, 15-inch**, paid upgrade. The 15-inch was Apple's first time bringing the larger panel to the Air line, and it became the size I refused to give back.
- **M4 MacBook Air, 15-inch**, current. Same form factor as the M2 because Apple barely changed the chassis, materially better silicon, and 16 GB as the new floor SKU.

## On the spec

For the way I actually use a laptop, writing, MDX, light dev, photo culling, the occasional Logic project, this config is overspecced. The M4 headroom matters mostly when Logic is running plug-ins or Final Cut is queueing an export. The 15-inch panel is the real reason this is the daily, not the chip: an extra 2 inches is the difference between split-screening a doc and a terminal vs. constant alt-tab. Anyone considering the 13 vs 15 split should know the only meaningful trade-off is weight, and after three years on the 15-inch I do not notice it.
