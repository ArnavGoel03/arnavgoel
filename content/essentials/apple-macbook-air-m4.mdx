Apple's 13-inch MacBook Air on the M4, the launch-year base config with 16 GB unified memory and 256 GB storage. Daily driver. Fanless, 18 hours of real-world battery on writing-and-browsing days, the same Liquid Retina display the M2 had. The one practical jump from the M3 is that 16 GB is now the floor instead of 8 GB.

For the way I use a laptop — writing, MDX, light dev, photo culling, a single Logic project on a slow afternoon — this is overspecced. The M4 boost matters mostly when Logic is running plug-ins or a Final Cut export is queued. Verdict on long-run heat behaviour and screen wear after a year of UCSD coffee-shop use, pending real notes.
