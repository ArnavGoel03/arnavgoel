Apple's USB-C AirPods Pro 2 with the H2 chip — the second-gen Pros after Apple swapped the original Lightning case for a MagSafe USB-C one. ANC is the headline; the conversation boost mode is the underrated feature. Adaptive Audio passes voices through while ducking everything else, which is the closest thing to "noise cancellation that doesn't isolate you from people" I've used.

Battery: ~6 hours per charge with ANC on, a couple of full charges from the case before it needs a cable, fits a full transcontinental flight if you reset between segments. Pair-once-everywhere works as advertised across the M4, an iPhone, and the iPad if I happen to be on the Vision Pro side of things. The hearing-aid-mode firmware update from 2024 is genuinely useful in loud cafes — the audiogram-driven amplification feels more like an accessibility tool than a gimmick.

Verdict pending real wear time on the silicone tips and battery degradation past a year. The known weak point on this generation is case scuffing more than the buds themselves.
