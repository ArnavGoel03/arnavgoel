Off-brand tempered glass with a dust-shield film around the speaker cutout, the small detail that distinguishes a $7 protector from a $30 one. Used on the iPhone 14 Pro Max during the year I had that phone before upgrading to the 16 Pro Max. The frame-assisted installer is the kind every screen protector should ship with: alignment is automatic, dust trapping is rare.

Replaced quarterly when the oleophobic coating wears, same as I do with the ESR pack on the 16 Pro Max. The reason this brand earns a spot is the price-per-pack: Amazon's no-name protectors ship 3 to 4 in a pack for under $10, and within that tier Fotbor's installation kit is a step above.
