The favourite. Toblerone Original, the triangular Swiss bar with the honey-and-almond nougat folded into the milk chocolate, exactly the way it has been since 1908. Not pretending this is a supplement (the Ghirardelli 86% bar gets to make that argument; this one does not). It is dessert, it is comfort, it is what I reach for when I need ten minutes that taste like a small win.

The 100 g bar is the right size: enough to share, small enough to not feel ridiculous if I do not. The triangular pieces snap off cleanly along the ridge, which is the small piece of industrial design that makes this the chocolate I keep buying when there are objectively fancier options on the same shelf.

Available everywhere, which is the other reason it stays in rotation. Airport newsagents, hotel minibars, every supermarket on three continents: there is always a Toblerone within reach.
