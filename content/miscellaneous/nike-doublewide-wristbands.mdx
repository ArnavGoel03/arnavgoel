Nike's DoubleWide wristband, the wide terry version sold for tennis, is what I actually use to keep water off my forearms during the morning double-cleanse. The CERETIA microfiber wristband-set was the first thing I tried for the splash-control problem, returned it, and then realised an athletic terry wristband does the same job better, lasts longer, and washes in the laundry without going limp.

## Why this works

The terry pile wicks water away from the wrist instead of soaking through. The wide cuff (about 4&nbsp;inches) covers the spot where soap-and-water actually runs, the inside of the forearm just past the watch line. The ribbed elastic stays put through fifteen seconds of vigorous oil-cleansing without sliding up.

## Why I picked Nike specifically

I had a pair already from playing tennis. They cost roughly the same as the CERETIA "spa" set ($15 vs ~$11) but they're built like sports gear — they survive a hot wash, a tumble dry, and three years of skin oil without the microfiber going crunchy.

## Caveats

They're black and grey, not aesthetic. They live by the sink in a small basket and look exactly like sports kit because they are sports kit. If you want the spa look, go with the CERETIA-style microfiber set; if you want it to actually work and last, this.
