## The family tree

All retinoids convert, in the skin, into **retinoic acid**, which is what actually does the work. How many conversion steps you skip determines how strong the product is and how irritating.

```
Retinyl Palmitate → Retinol → Retinal → Retinoic Acid (tretinoin)
(weakest)                                          (strongest)
```

Each step roughly 10× more potent and faster-acting than the one before.

## What each one means in practice

- **Retinyl palmitate**, an ester. Marketed in "gentle" products; effectively near-zero active once it reaches the skin. Skip unless your skin reacts to everything stronger.
- **Retinol**, the standard OTC retinoid. 0.25-1% is the useful range. Takes 3-6 months to see meaningful changes. Irritation during the first 2-4 weeks is normal; if it doesn't settle, ramp slower.
- **Retinal (retinaldehyde)**, one conversion step from retinoic acid. Noticeably faster than retinol at comparable irritation levels. Harder to find, usually more expensive.
- **Tretinoin (retinoic acid)**, prescription in most countries. The real thing; the gold standard for acne, photoaging, and texture. 2-4× faster than retinol, proportionally more irritating at the start.
- **Granactive retinoid (HPR)**, a newer ester. Less peer-reviewed data, vendor-driven marketing. Genuinely lower irritation, efficacy claims not yet settled.

## The ramp

Regardless of which you pick:

1. **Start 2×/week** for the first 2 weeks.
2. **Move to 3×/week** for weeks 3-4.
3. **Nightly only after your skin tolerates it without visible flaking.**
4. SPF every morning. Non-negotiable. Retinoids thin the outer layer of dead skin; sun damage without SPF defeats the point.

## Pair with

- A **fragrance-free moisturizer** with ceramides, retinoid nights are when the barrier works hardest.
- A **gentle cleanser**. No foaming tightening cleansers on retinoid nights.
- Never mix retinoid + AHA/BHA + Vitamin C on the same evening. Rotate.
