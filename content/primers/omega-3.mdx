## The two compounds that matter

Omega-3 is a family. For supplementation you care about two members:

- **EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid)** — inflammation and cardiovascular effects
- **DHA (docosahexaenoic acid)** — brain, eyes, retinal health

ALA (the plant-source one, from flax and chia) converts to EPA/DHA in humans at ~5% efficiency. It's not a substitute.

## What to look for on a label

1. **Combined EPA + DHA dose**, not total fish oil. A 1,000 mg capsule might have only 300 mg of actual omega-3. The number to compare is the EPA + DHA sum.
2. **Triglyceride (TG) or re-esterified triglyceride (rTG) form** over ethyl ester (EE). Better absorbed, less burping.
3. **Third-party tested** for oxidation (IFOS, USP) and heavy metals.
4. **Sustainable source.** Wild Alaska pollock, sardines, or anchovies — not farmed salmon or generic "fish."

## Typical dosing

- **General wellness**: 500–1,000 mg EPA + DHA / day
- **Cardiovascular**: 1,000–2,000 mg / day (talked-about floor in most trials)
- **Post-workout inflammation / joint**: 2,000–3,000 mg / day

Take with the largest fatty meal of the day — absorption triples.

## The burping problem

Fish-oil burp almost always means the oil is **oxidized**. Rancid fish oil is worse than no fish oil — it's pro-inflammatory. Refrigerate after opening; replace bottles older than ~90 days once they're open.
