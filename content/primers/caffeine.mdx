## What it does, briefly

Caffeine is an adenosine antagonist. Adenosine builds up in your brain across the waking day and, when it binds its receptors, makes you feel tired. Caffeine occupies those receptors without activating them, which is why you feel alert, not energized. The tiredness is still building; you just can't feel it.

It also mildly increases dopamine and norepinephrine release. Net effect: more focus, slight mood lift, faster reaction time, measurably better endurance (about 3-7% improvement at 3-6 mg/kg bodyweight before exercise).

## Half-life math

The half-life of caffeine in adults is **4 to 6 hours** for most people, **8+ hours** for slow metabolizers (CYP1A2 slow variants, pregnancy, some medications like oral contraceptives).

What that means in practice:

- A 200 mg coffee at **2 pm** means you still have **~50 mg** in your system at **10 pm**. Enough to degrade deep-sleep architecture even if you fall asleep fine.
- A 200 mg coffee at **noon** means you have **~25 mg** at **10 pm**. Much better.
- A 200 mg coffee at **8 am** means ~6 mg at 10 pm. No practical effect on sleep.

The last-caffeine cutoff that preserves sleep quality, for most people:

- Average metabolizer: **8-10 hours before bed**.
- Fast metabolizer: 6 hours is often fine.
- Slow metabolizer: 12+ hours, which means morning-only.

If you're not sure which you are, assume average.

## The L-theanine pairing

L-theanine (an amino acid from tea leaves) partially offsets caffeine's sympathetic activation while preserving the focus benefit. The result:

- Less jittery feeling
- Lower heart-rate response
- Smoother alpha-wave activity (the "calm focus" quality)

**Ratio: 1:2 caffeine to L-theanine.** 100 mg caffeine + 200 mg L-theanine is the most-studied dose. Some people prefer 100:100 for a cleaner caffeine feel without full dampening.

Fun fact: matcha naturally provides this ratio because the tea leaves contain both. Espresso doesn't. If you want the smoothness without supplements, drink good matcha.

## Tolerance

Daily caffeine users develop substantial receptor upregulation within 2-4 weeks. The subjective "alertness boost" fades; you're mostly staving off the withdrawal headache that would otherwise appear by noon.

Options:

1. **Cycle off.** Skip caffeine on weekends or take a 2-week break every 3 months. Full receptor density returns in about 7-10 days.
2. **Stay low-dose.** 50-100 mg/day (one small coffee) barely builds tolerance; the acute effect mostly returns.
3. **Accept the floor.** If you drink 400 mg/day daily, accept you're paying caffeine rent. You still get effects, they're just smaller than first-dose.

## Withdrawal

Skipping caffeine after regular use produces, within 12-24 hours:

- Headache (most common)
- Fatigue and slight depression
- Difficulty concentrating

Peaks at 24-48 hours, resolves within 3-7 days. Severity tracks with daily intake. If you're a 4-coffee-a-day person considering quitting, ramp down over 2 weeks instead of cold-turkey.

## Safe upper bound

**400 mg/day** for most healthy adults is the FDA's general guidance. **200 mg/day** during pregnancy. Above 600 mg/day is where adverse-event rates climb meaningfully: arrhythmia, anxiety, insomnia, GI discomfort.

Rough caffeine content:

- Brewed coffee (8 oz): 80-120 mg
- Espresso shot: 65 mg
- Matcha (1 tsp): 60-80 mg
- Green tea: 25-40 mg
- Pre-workout scoop: 150-300 mg (check labels; some are absurd)
- Energy drinks: 80-300 mg

## What to pair with it

- **L-theanine**, per the ratio above, if you find caffeine jittery.
- **Creatine**, if you train. No direct interaction, just both sit in the performance stack.
- **Food.** Caffeine on empty stomach spikes cortisol more than with breakfast. If you're stress-prone, eat first.

## What not to pair with it

- **Alcohol**, as a regular pattern. Both disrupt sleep architecture; stacked effects are larger than the sum.
- **Stimulant medication (ADHD meds)** without talking to your prescriber. Usually fine in moderation, occasionally not.
- **Late-afternoon coffee** if you're serious about sleep. No magic beans.
