## What they all do

All three acid families loosen the bonds between dead skin cells in the stratum corneum, speeding desquamation (the shedding of old cells). Faster turnover produces:

- Smoother texture
- Less dullness
- Fewer clogged pores
- Faster fading of post-inflammatory marks
- Better product penetration of what comes after

The differences are in **size** (which determines how deep they go), **polarity** (oil-soluble vs water-soluble, which determines whether they clear pores), and **irritation profile**.

## AHAs (alpha-hydroxy acids): water-soluble, surface-level

Derived from fruit or milk. Work on the skin surface; don't penetrate pores. Best for:

- Dullness
- Textural roughness
- Surface hyperpigmentation
- Fine lines

### Glycolic acid
- The smallest AHA. Penetrates deepest among AHAs.
- Most research support; most results; most irritation.
- Use at 5-10% to start; 15-20% for experienced users.
- Can cause redness and increased sun sensitivity. Always pair with daily SPF.

### Lactic acid
- Gentler, larger molecule. Also humectant (hydrating).
- Good for dry and sensitive skin that can't tolerate glycolic.
- 5-10% is a solid workhorse strength.

### Mandelic acid
- Much larger molecule; most gentle AHA.
- Antibacterial; popular for acne-prone skin.
- 5-10% is typical. Slower visible results than glycolic, less risk.

## BHA: oil-soluble, goes into pores

### Salicylic acid
- The only BHA most people will encounter.
- Oil-soluble, which means it penetrates and clears sebum-filled pores.
- Best for:
  - Blackheads and clogged pores
  - Oily skin
  - Comedonal (non-inflammatory) acne
  - Keratosis pilaris on body
- Mild anti-inflammatory action (it's related to aspirin).
- Typical leave-on strength: 1-2%. Cleanser strength: 0.5-2%.

Combine AHA + BHA approach: AHA for surface smoothness, BHA for pore clarity. Plenty of products layer them.

## PHA: polyhydroxy acids, biggest and gentlest

### Gluconolactone and lactobionic acid
- Large molecules. Don't penetrate deeply; exfoliate the very top layer.
- Humectant and mildly antioxidant.
- Best for:
  - Very sensitive skin (rosacea, eczema-adjacent)
  - Compromised barriers post-procedure
  - Retinoid newbies who still want exfoliation
- Typical strength: 5-10% without significant irritation.

## Which acid for which problem

- **Blackheads, pore clarity, oiliness:** Salicylic acid 1-2%.
- **Dullness, textural smoothing, PIH:** Glycolic acid 5-10% or lactic acid 10%.
- **Sensitive or rosacea-prone:** Mandelic acid 5-10%, or PHA.
- **Body (KP on upper arms, rough heels):** Glycolic acid 10-15% lotion, or urea 10%.
- **Melasma:** Lactic or mandelic for gentleness; pair with tranexamic acid, niacinamide, and strict SPF.

## Frequency

- **Beginners:** 1-2 times per week on alternating evenings. Build up.
- **Maintenance:** 2-4 times per week for most people.
- **Daily:** Only if your skin has adapted and you're not also using retinoid nightly.

## What to not do

- **Don't layer AHA/BHA with retinoid on the same night.** Alternate evenings.
- **Don't layer AHA/BHA with vitamin C on the same morning** unless your barrier is very robust. The combined low pH can irritate.
- **Don't use acids and scrub.** Chemical + physical exfoliation back-to-back is how people destroy their barriers.
- **Don't skip sunscreen on exfoliation days.** Newly exfoliated skin is UV-sensitive.

## Signs you're overdoing it

- Skin feels tight and stings after cleansing.
- Redness that persists more than an hour.
- Flaking beyond mild shed.
- "Glass skin" that photographs well but feels paper-thin: you're over-exfoliated.

Stop all acids for 1-2 weeks. Simplify to cleanser + barrier moisturizer + SPF. Let the barrier rebuild. Then reintroduce at lower frequency.

## Leave-on vs rinse-off

- **Leave-on serums and toners:** most effective. Contact time matters.
- **Cleansers and peels:** shorter contact; milder effect per use. Good for beginners.

## Pairings that work

- **Niacinamide** after your acid step to calm and hydrate.
- **Moisturizer with ceramides** to rebuild the barrier you just exfoliated.
- **SPF next morning**, non-negotiable.

- Paula's Choice 2% BHA Liquid is the benchmark salicylic acid product.
