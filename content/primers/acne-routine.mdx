## The three tools that actually work OTC

Over-the-counter acne care is built around three ingredients. Everything else is marketing, mostly.

### 1. Salicylic acid (BHA, 0.5-2%)
- Penetrates sebum-clogged pores.
- Best for **blackheads, whiteheads, and comedonal acne**.
- Daily use is fine once tolerated.
- Forms: cleansers (short contact), toners/serums (leave-on, more effective).

### 2. Benzoyl peroxide (2.5-10%)
- Kills C. acnes bacteria directly. Pores don't build resistance the way they do to antibiotics.
- Best for **inflammatory acne** (red papules, pustules).
- **2.5% works as well as 10%** in most trials, with far less irritation.
- Bleaches fabrics permanently. Warn anyone who shares a towel with you.

### 3. Adapalene 0.1% (retinoid, OTC in US since 2016)
- The mildest prescription retinoid, now OTC.
- Normalizes keratinization, prevents new comedones, reduces inflammation.
- Best for **overall maintenance and chronic acne**.
- Has a 6-week "purging" window where it can worsen before it improves. Push through.

## Typical routine structure

### Morning
- Gentle cleanser (not foaming if possible).
- Niacinamide 5-10% (regulates sebum, reduces redness).
- Light, non-comedogenic moisturizer.
- SPF (non-negotiable on an active acne routine).

### Evening
- Cleanse (double if wearing SPF / makeup).
- **One active** (rotate; see below).
- Light moisturizer.

## The rotation

The fastest way to destroy acne-prone skin is to layer three strong actives on the same night. Rotate:

- **Mon/Wed/Fri:** Adapalene (nightly once tolerated).
- **Tue/Thu:** Salicylic acid or azelaic acid.
- **Sat/Sun:** Rest and barrier repair, unless you're using adapalene nightly.

Benzoyl peroxide sits outside this schedule. If you need it for active inflammatory breakouts:

- **Morning:** BP as a spot treatment or thin layer on affected area.
- **Evening:** Whatever your rotation calls for.
- **Never same time as adapalene**: BP oxidizes some retinoids. Different times of day is fine.

## Spot treatments

- **Hydrocolloid patches** (Mighty Patch, CosRx Pimple Master): for already-formed pustules. Pull fluid out overnight, keep fingers off, prevent scarring.
- **BP 2.5-5% dab** on emerging papules: apply at first signs.
- **Ice** on a deep cyst: reduces inflammation before anything else.

Don't pop. Don't dig. Post-inflammatory marks from squeezed acne take 3-6 months to fade; a pimple itself is a 5-day problem.

## What to avoid

- **Oil cleansing (if you're acne-prone)**: mixed evidence, some people break out.
- **Fragranced moisturizers**: not technically comedogenic but they do irritate acne-prone skin.
- **Scrubs and physical exfoliation**: makes inflammation worse.
- **"Clarifying" masks that leave skin tight**: strips the barrier, triggers rebound oil production.
- **DIY toothpaste spot treatments**: burns skin. Skin irritation is not a treatment.

## Niacinamide's role

Underrated ingredient for acne-prone skin. At 5-10% it:

- Reduces sebum production (modestly but consistently)
- Calms inflammation
- Fades post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
- Plays well with every other acne active
- Almost never irritates

Layer it in the morning, on top of moisturizer, before SPF. Can also use at night on rest days.

## Diet and lifestyle

Limited but real evidence for dietary effects:

- **High-glycemic-index foods** correlate with acne severity in controlled studies.
- **Skim milk** specifically (more than whole milk) associates with acne flares in some people.
- **Whey protein** can trigger breakouts in acne-prone individuals.

These are triggers, not causes. If you've eliminated dairy for 3 months without improvement, dairy wasn't your driver.

Sleep, stress, and hormonal cycles matter more than most diet interventions.

## When to see a dermatologist

- Cystic acne (deep, painful lumps that don't drain)
- Scarring from active acne
- No improvement after 3 months of correct OTC routine
- Acne covering back, chest, and face simultaneously
- Adult-onset hormonal acne with cyclical pattern

Prescription options (tretinoin, oral antibiotics, spironolactone, isotretinoin) are significantly stronger than OTC. Don't suffer through years of OTC failure when a dermatology visit costs less than six months of failed products.

## Realistic timeline

- **Salicylic acid:** 2-4 weeks for comedonal improvement.
- **Benzoyl peroxide:** 1-2 weeks for inflammatory lesions.
- **Adapalene:** 6 weeks initial worsening, 3 months for substantial improvement, 6 months for peak benefit.

Stick with a routine for 3 full months before judging whether it works.

## The minimum viable acne routine

1. Gentle cleanser AM + PM.
2. Adapalene 0.1% PM.
3. Light moisturizer AM + PM.
4. SPF AM.

Everything else is optimization on top.
