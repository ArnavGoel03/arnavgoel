## What the morning is for

Morning skincare is defensive. The job is to protect skin from the UV, oxidation, and pollution it's about to meet, not to transform it. Four steps, fewer actives than evening, heavier on sunscreen.

## The sequence

### 1. Cleanse or rinse

You woke up in a clean pillowcase. Unless you sweated through the night, oiled your face, or used retinoid / acid that needs rinsing off, **water is enough**.

If you want a cleanser: a low-foaming, non-stripping gel or milk. La Roche-Posay Toleriane, Cetaphil Gentle Cleanser, Cerave Hydrating are all appropriate.

Skip: foaming cleansers with sulfates, anything described as "deep cleansing," cleansing with hot water. Strip the barrier here and every product downstream has less to work with.

### 2. Antioxidant serum

The one meaningful active in the morning. Vitamin C is the canonical choice (10-20% L-ascorbic acid, or 5-10% THD for sensitive skin). Alternatives:

- **Niacinamide 5-10%**: milder, broader benefits, stackable with everything.
- **Resveratrol or ferulic acid** alongside vitamin C for enhanced stability.

The antioxidant's job is to neutralize reactive oxygen species generated by UV and pollution. It works with, not instead of, sunscreen.

Apply to clean skin while it's slightly damp (better penetration). Give it 60-90 seconds to absorb before the next step.

### 3. Moisturizer

Even if you have oily skin. Hydrated skin is less prone to compensatory oil overproduction.

- **Oily / combination:** Light gel. Anua Rice 70 Moisturizing Milk, Cerave PM Facial (also works AM), TIRTIR Milk Skin.
- **Normal:** Cream-to-gel hybrid.
- **Dry:** Richer cream with ceramides. La Roche-Posay Lipikar, CeraVe Moisturizing Cream.

Wait 30-60 seconds before SPF.

### 4. Sunscreen

**Two fingers' worth**, face and neck, every morning, SPF 30 or above, broad-spectrum.

This is the most important step in the routine. See the Sunscreen 101 primer for the full explanation of why.

If you wear makeup, let sunscreen set for 2-3 minutes before applying anything over it.

## What not to do in the morning

- **Strong AHAs/BHAs.** You'll compound UV sensitivity. Save for evening.
- **Retinoids.** Photosensitizing (tretinoin) or photounstable (most retinols). Evening only.
- **Layering 5+ products.** Most of them won't absorb, and the ones that do will interfere with each other.
- **Skipping sunscreen because "I'm not going outside."** UVA penetrates windows. You're also likely stepping outside at some point, even briefly.

## The time budget

The full routine, done honestly: 3-5 minutes. Add another minute if you use makeup.

If you can't commit to that, reduce to: **rinse, moisturizer with built-in SPF (Anua, La Roche-Posay, EltaMD all make these), done**. A two-minute routine beats an aspirational five-minute one that gets skipped twice a week.

## Combining with makeup

- **Primer:** unnecessary if your sunscreen is well-formulated. Most sunscreen double as primer.
- **Foundation:** apply after SPF has had 2-3 minutes to set.
- **Powder SPF for touchups:** fine as reapplication, not as primary coverage.

## The minimum viable morning

If you do only two things in the morning:

1. Moisturize
2. Sunscreen

Everything else is optimization on top of those two.
