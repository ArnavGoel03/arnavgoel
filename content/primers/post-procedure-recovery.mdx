## When you need this routine

- 2-7 days after a chemical peel (glycolic, TCA, Jessner).
- 1-14 days after laser resurfacing (fractional, full ablative).
- The week after a bad retinoid reaction or acid over-use.
- Anytime your skin feels tight, stings on application, and shows patches of redness or peeling that don't calm down with a normal routine.
- After extractions or any procedure that leaves raw skin.

The protocol is the same regardless of cause: **strip everything non-essential, support lipids, occlude water loss, and block UV.** For as long as it takes the barrier to rebuild.

## The 4-product recovery routine

### 1. Gentle, non-foaming cleanser

Or rinse with lukewarm water if even gentle cleanser stings.

Appropriate: La Roche-Posay Toleriane Dermo-Cleanser, Bioderma Hydrabio H2O micellar water, Cetaphil Gentle Cleanser, Avène Tolérance Extrême Cleanser.

**Avoid:** foaming cleansers, sulfates, anything marketed as "deep clean," hot water, washcloths, Clarisonic-type brushes.

### 2. Ceramide-rich moisturizer

Thick, barrier-focused, fragrance-free. Use 2-3 times per day, not just AM/PM.

Appropriate:
- La Roche-Posay Lipikar Baume AP+M
- CeraVe Healing Ointment or Moisturizing Cream
- Dr. Jart+ Ceramidin Cream
- Avène Tolérance Extrême Emulsion
- Skinfix Triple Lipid Barrier Cream

### 3. Occlusive

A thin layer of petrolatum (or petrolatum-blend) on top of moisturizer, especially at night and any time the skin feels tight.

- Vaseline Original (pure petrolatum) is the cheapest and most effective.
- Aquaphor Healing Ointment is petrolatum + lanolin + glycerin; smoother, gentler.
- CeraVe Healing Ointment is similar to Aquaphor with ceramides.

The "slugging" technique. Don't skip it during active recovery.

### 4. Sunscreen

**Mineral only** during recovery (chemical filters can sting broken skin). Zinc oxide, titanium dioxide, or both. SPF 30+.

Appropriate:
- La Roche-Posay Anthelios Mineral Tinted
- EltaMD UV Pure Broad-Spectrum SPF 47
- Blue Lizard Sensitive Mineral Sunscreen

Apply even if you're staying indoors near windows. UVA penetrates glass and compromised skin burns faster.

## What to avoid for 1-2 weeks (or longer)

- **All actives:** retinoid, AHA, BHA, PHA, vitamin C, exfoliants of any kind.
- **Fragranced products.**
- **Essential oils** ("natural" does not mean gentle).
- **Masks** (except simple hydrating sheet masks, and only if fragrance-free).
- **Makeup on active recovery zones** for at least 3-5 days.
- **Hot showers on the face.** Warm, short, rinse face separately with lukewarm.
- **Alcohol-based toners and astringents.**

## What helps beyond products

- **Ice-cold water or cold compresses** for stinging / heat.
- **Sleep on your back** with a silk pillowcase. Friction on healing skin makes things worse.
- **Drink water.** Dehydrated skin heals slower.
- **Avoid sun exposure completely** for at least the first 5-7 days post-peel or laser.
- **Don't pick, peel, or scrub.** Dead skin will shed on its own schedule.

## Reintroduction schedule

Once your skin is:
- Not stinging
- Not flaking
- Not red after cleansing
- Back to normal texture

You can start layering back in, one product per week:

- **Week 1 back:** add gentle hydrating serum (hyaluronic acid).
- **Week 2 back:** add a low-dose active, once a week. Niacinamide, azelaic acid, or the gentlest retinoid you were using before.
- **Week 3-4 back:** rebuild frequency slowly. Most people start stronger than they should.

If anything stings or irritates, back off another week. Your skin just told you it wasn't ready.

## Post-peel / post-laser specific notes

- **Days 1-3:** stinging, redness, tightness are expected.
- **Days 3-7:** peeling or flaking is normal for peels. Don't pick.
- **Days 5-14:** new skin is photosensitive for up to 6 months. SPF every single day.
- **Week 2 onward:** introduce gentle hydrating products; avoid actives for 3-4 weeks post-peel, 6-12 weeks post-laser.

## What a recovery routine isn't

- A quick fix. Compromised barriers take 14-28 days to fully rebuild.
- A permanent routine. Once recovered, you can (and should) reintroduce actives.
- A reason to avoid seeing a dermatologist if you have a burn, infection, or reaction that's worsening rather than improving.

## When to escalate

Call your dermatologist or doctor if:
- Swelling or blistering
- Yellow or green discharge
- Fever alongside skin reaction
- Pain that's worsening past day 3
- Any sign of infection

Post-procedure redness is expected. Infection is not.
