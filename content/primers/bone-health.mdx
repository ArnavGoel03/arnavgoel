## The one-line summary

Calcium alone is a waste of money. The stack is **Calcium + D3 + K2 (MK-7) + Magnesium**, each piece fixes the others' failure mode.

## What each one does

**Calcium** is the building block. Without enough of it, bone density declines. Dietary calcium (dairy, leafy greens, tofu, sardines) is preferred; supplement only to close the gap.

**Vitamin D3** is required for your gut to absorb calcium in the first place. Serum 25(OH)D below ~30 ng/mL compromises absorption. Most people supplement 1,000-2,000 IU/day, higher if blood levels are low.

**Vitamin K2 (specifically MK-7)** directs calcium *into* bones and teeth and away from arteries. This is the piece most "bone formulas" skip. Without K2, supplemented calcium has been associated with arterial calcification in some trials. MK-7 over MK-4 because the half-life is ~70 hours vs 1 hour.

**Magnesium** is a cofactor for the enzyme that activates vitamin D. Low magnesium means D3 doesn't convert well, which means calcium doesn't get absorbed. Glycinate is the friendliest form.

## What to skip

- Calcium carbonate alone. Cheap, poorly absorbed, often constipating.
- "Bone formulas" that list calcium and D3 but no K2.
- Mega-doses of any single piece. The ratio matters more than the size.
