## The premise

Most "longevity" marketing chases compounds with interesting mechanism and weak human evidence (NMN, resveratrol, urolithin A). The short list of things that consistently move cardiovascular markers, all-cause mortality, or both in large human trials is boring, cheap, and old.

## The core five

### 1. Omega-3 (EPA + DHA, 2 to 4 g combined / day)

The most-studied cardiovascular supplement. REDUCE-IT and similar trials show meaningful reductions in major adverse cardiovascular events at higher doses. Benefits scale with dose up to about 4 g/day combined EPA+DHA.

Specific effects:
- Lower fasting triglycerides (30 to 50 percent reductions at 4 g)
- Slight blood pressure reduction
- Anti-inflammatory shift across IL-6 and CRP

Buy triglyceride-form, third-party tested for mercury/PCBs (IFOS or USP verified). Prescription icosapent ethyl (Vascepa) is the most concentrated EPA-only form if your doctor is willing.

### 2. CoQ10 (100-200 mg / day, ubiquinol form after age 40)

Mitochondrial cofactor, essential for ATP synthesis in heart muscle specifically. Evidence strongest in:

- People on statins (statins deplete endogenous CoQ10)
- Congestive heart failure (Q-SYMBIO trial, meaningful mortality signal)
- Anyone 50+ with cardiovascular risk factors

Ubiquinol is the reduced, active form. Younger adults can convert ubiquinone fine; past 40 or on statins, pay for ubiquinol. Take with a fat-containing meal.

### 3. Vitamin K2 (MK-7, 100-200 mcg / day)

Directs calcium. K2 activates matrix Gla-protein, which pulls calcium out of arterial walls and into bone. Populations with higher K2 intake have measurably lower arterial calcification and cardiovascular mortality (Rotterdam Study).

MK-7 is the long-acting form. Take with a fat-containing meal and with vitamin D3 (they act synergistically on calcium trafficking).

### 4. Magnesium (300-400 mg elemental / day)

Population-level magnesium deficiency is common and correlates with hypertension, arrhythmia, and insulin resistance. Supplementation at 300 to 400 mg elemental per day:

- Modestly lowers blood pressure (4 to 5 mmHg systolic)
- Reduces type-2 diabetes incidence in at-risk populations
- Improves sleep depth (indirect cardiovascular benefit)

Glycinate for tolerability. Don't use magnesium oxide (poorly absorbed) for this purpose.

### 5. Vitamin D3 (2,000-4,000 IU / day, adjust to a 25-OH-D level of 40-60 ng/mL)

The trials are mixed on whether vitamin D supplementation prevents disease in D-sufficient people. But if you live above 35 degrees latitude, work indoors, or have darker skin, you are probably insufficient. Bringing a deficient person to sufficiency has a meaningful cardiovascular and mortality signal.

Test your 25-hydroxyvitamin D level. Target 40 to 60 ng/mL (100-150 nmol/L). Most people need 2,000 to 4,000 IU/day with a fat-containing meal. Pair with K2.

## The optional layer

- **Taurine (2-3 g / day)**. Interesting animal mortality data and a small number of positive human cardiovascular trials. Cheap, safe, emerging.
- **Curcumin (500 mg with piperine or Meriva formulation)**. Decent anti-inflammatory signal. Skip if you're on anticoagulants.
- **Allicin-standardized garlic extract (600-1200 mg)**. Modest blood pressure and lipid effects.

## What to skip (for now)

- **NMN, NR, NAD+ precursors.** Interesting science, thin human trials, expensive.
- **Resveratrol.** The human data did not live up to the wine-and-mice hype.
- **Urolithin A.** Early days. Let it get 10 more years of trials before paying $50/month.
- **Berberine.** Works for blood sugar, but has the effect size and pharmacokinetic profile of metformin, which is better-understood and cheaper. If you'd consider berberine, talk to your doctor about metformin instead.

## What outperforms the stack

1. Zone 2 cardio, 150+ minutes/week, plus 1 to 2 sessions of higher-intensity intervals.
2. Resistance training, 2 to 3 times per week. Muscle mass is the single best predictor of healthspan past 60.
3. Sleep 7 to 9 hours.
4. Don't smoke. Drink little, or not at all.
5. Keep LDL and blood pressure in range. A statin or antihypertensive at the right time of life does more than any supplement on this page.
