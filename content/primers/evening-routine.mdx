## What the evening is for

Evening skincare is transformation. This is when actives (retinoids, acids, exfoliants, targeted treatments) actually do their work. The barrier is at rest, you're not about to meet UV, and skin cell turnover happens overnight.

Core principle: **one strong active per night**. Mixing retinoid, strong AHA, and benzoyl peroxide on the same evening is how barriers get destroyed.

## The sequence

### 1. Double cleanse (or single if minimal makeup / sunscreen)

**Step 1, oil cleanser** (balm or oil): dissolves sunscreen, makeup, sebum. Massage onto dry skin, emulsify with a little water, rinse.

**Step 2, water cleanser** (gel, foam, milk): removes what the oil loosened. Gentle is fine here; the hard work was step 1.

If you wore no makeup and only light sunscreen: a single gentle cleanser is enough.

### 2. Treatment active (rotate, don't stack)

Pick **one** based on the night:

#### Retinoid nights (2-3x per week for beginners, nightly once tolerated)
- Tretinoin (prescription), adapalene 0.1% (OTC), retinol/retinal (OTC).
- Pea-sized amount for entire face.
- Apply to dry skin (damp skin amplifies irritation).
- Wait 15-20 minutes before moisturizer if you're tolerant; apply moisturizer immediately on top (buffer) if sensitive.

#### Acid nights (1-3x per week depending on tolerance)
- AHA (glycolic 5-10%, lactic 5-10%) or BHA (salicylic 1-2%).
- Never on the same night as retinoid.
- Follow with hydrating + barrier support.

#### Treatment serums (targeted, nightly or as needed)
- Azelaic acid for rosacea / PIH / mild acne.
- Niacinamide for barrier, oil regulation.
- Tranexamic acid for melasma.
- Gentler than retinoid or acids; can share nights with neither.

### 3. Hydrating layer (optional but useful)

Hyaluronic acid serum or essence. Apply to damp skin after the active has absorbed. Don't skip this if you live in a dry climate or use retinoid.

### 4. Barrier-repair moisturizer

Heavier than the morning moisturizer. Ceramides + cholesterol + fatty acids in a 1:1:1 ratio if possible.

- **Dry, compromised barrier:** La Roche-Posay Lipikar, CeraVe Moisturizing Cream.
- **Normal:** Dr. Jart+ Ceramidin Cream, Anua Rice 70.
- **Oily / acne-prone:** TIRTIR Ceramic Milk or Anua Rice 70 (lighter, still barrier-focused).

### 5. Occlusive (optional, "slugging")

On dry weeks or the night after strong actives: a thin layer of petrolatum or petrolatum-blend occlusive on top of moisturizer. Vaseline, Aquaphor, or CeraVe Healing Ointment work.

Slug only on non-active nights; it'll trap retinoid and increase irritation otherwise.

## Layering rules

- **Retinoid + acid on the same night:** never. Alternate evenings.
- **Retinoid + vitamin C:** different times. Retinoid PM, vitamin C AM.
- **Retinoid + niacinamide:** compatible and synergistic. Niacinamide buffers some retinoid irritation.
- **Strong acids + exfoliating scrubs:** redundant at best, barrier-destroying at worst. Pick one.
- **Benzoyl peroxide + retinoid:** alternate mornings/evenings or different days. BP oxidizes many retinoids.

## Weekly structure example

**Beginner (first 2-3 months):**

- Mon: Retinoid
- Tue: Hydrate only
- Wed: Acid
- Thu: Hydrate only
- Fri: Retinoid
- Sat: Rest (barrier repair)
- Sun: Acid

**Intermediate (after retinization):**

- Mon/Wed/Fri: Retinoid
- Tue/Thu: Acid or treatment
- Sat/Sun: Hydrate and rest

**Advanced (fully adapted):**

- Retinoid nightly, acids 1-2x weekly.

## When to rest

If your skin is:
- Flaking beyond mild shed
- Red and sensitive longer than an hour
- Stinging with products that normally don't sting
- Feeling tight and brittle

Stop all actives for 5-7 nights. Cleanse gently, moisturize heavily, barrier repair only. Restart at a lower frequency.

## The minimum viable evening

If you only do two things at night:

1. Cleanse (single or double).
2. Barrier-repair moisturizer.

Actives are optimization on top of cleansing and moisturizing. Don't layer actives onto a routine without those two foundations.
