## The TL;DR

Different magnesium salts target different tissues. Picking the wrong one is the reason most people say "magnesium didn't do anything for me."

| Form | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Glycinate** (bisglycinate) | Sleep, anxiety, daily use | Gentle on the stomach, not laxative |
| **L-Threonate** (Magtein) | Cognition, memory, brain fog | The only form that crosses the blood-brain barrier in meaningful amounts |
| **Citrate** | Constipation, digestion | Draws water into the bowel, helpful or inconvenient depending |
| **Malate** | Fatigue, muscle soreness | Some evidence for fibromyalgia and post-exercise recovery |
| **Oxide** | Nothing, really | ~4% bioavailable. Cheap, and you get what you pay for. |

## Which one do you pick

- **Default daily magnesium**: glycinate. Covers sleep, stress, muscle recovery, and you can take 400-600 mg without GI issues.
- **Brain-focused stack**: add L-threonate in the evening alongside glycinate. The research is newer but consistent; Magtein is the only clinically-studied version.
- **Skip oxide.** Even in a pill-organizer multivitamin, magnesium oxide is a rounding error.

## Don't stack blindly

Total elemental magnesium across all supplements should usually stay under ~800 mg/day for adults. If a multivitamin already has 200 mg, your glycinate serving should drop accordingly.
