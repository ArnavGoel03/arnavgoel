OTC sleep aid, doxylamine succinate at 25 mg per tab, the same first-generation antihistamine used in NyQuil's PM line. Sold as a "fall asleep" rather than a "stay asleep" pill; mechanism is just blocking the histamine receptors that keep the brain awake, which is why it knocks you down and why the next morning feels the way it does.

Returned within three weeks. The hangover was the headline problem: every dose left a foggy, slightly drugged feeling that did not lift until late morning, and the night-of sleep itself felt more like sedation than recovery. Cottonmouth on the dose, then a hard wake-up bolted to a sluggish first half of the day. The only thing it had going for it was the price; that's not enough.

For perspective: low-dose doxylamine is a real tool for an occasional bad night, but as anything close to a recurring sleep stack it does more harm than good. Magnesium glycinate at night does meaningfully better here without the residue.
