## Why monohydrate is the only form worth paying for

Creatine has been studied more than any other sports supplement. The form with virtually all the evidence behind it is **creatine monohydrate**, the cheapest form. The "advanced" forms (HCL, ethyl ester, nitrate, Kre-Alkalyn) don't outperform it in controlled trials; they just cost more.

Pay for monohydrate. Don't pay for marketing.

## What micronized means

Micronized just means the particles are ground finer so the powder dissolves more evenly in water. For capsules, it mostly matters for consistent dosing and mouthfeel when cracked open, not a performance difference in the body.

## Dose

The standard protocol is **3-5 g per day, every day**. No loading phase required (the "load then maintain" approach just saturates muscle stores ~2 weeks faster; the eventual outcome is the same). Consistency matters far more than timing, take it with whatever meal you're most likely not to skip.

Four Nutricost capsules = 3 g creatine monohydrate. One of the few supplements where dose-matters more than brand.

## What it actually does

Well-documented in trained populations:
- Increases phosphocreatine stores → more reps near max effort
- Modest increase in muscle water content (temporary "fuller" look in the first 2-3 weeks)
- Small but consistent strength and hypertrophy gains when paired with training

Emerging (weaker) evidence for cognitive effects in vegetarians and during sleep deprivation, the brain also uses creatine, but most diets already provide enough for non-training cognitive baselines.
