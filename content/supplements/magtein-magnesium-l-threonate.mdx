## Why a different form of magnesium

Most magnesium forms (glycinate, citrate, oxide) don't cross the blood-brain barrier in meaningful amounts. L-threonate is the form that does, the original research came out of MIT and showed measurable increases in brain magnesium levels in animal models, with cognitive correlates.

For the body, sleep, muscle recovery, GI, glycinate is still the right call. L-threonate is specifically for the brain story: memory, focus, cognitive resilience.

## Routine

Three capsules in the early evening, several hours before bed. Taking it at night seems to deepen sleep without making me drowsy at the time of dose.
