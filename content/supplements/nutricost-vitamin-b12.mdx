## Why supplement B12

If you eat meat regularly, you probably don't need to. If you're vegetarian or vegan, deficiency creeps up over years and the symptoms (fatigue, brain fog, neurological tingling) are easy to attribute to something else. Standard advice: supplement if your diet doesn't reliably include B12-rich animal products.

## Why methylcobalamin

The cheaper cyanocobalamin form requires your body to convert it to the active methyl form. Most people do this fine. Some people (with MTHFR variants) don't. Methylcobalamin skips the step. The cost difference is negligible at this dose.

## Routine

One capsule every other day. 2000 mcg is well above the daily requirement, and B12 stores in the liver, so daily isn't necessary.
