## What this is

Myprotein's Impact Whey is a **concentrate**, ~80% protein with a bit of residual lactose and fat. Cheaper per gram than isolate, slightly less protein per scoop, slightly more lactose.

For most people without a lactose issue, the price-per-gram advantage wins. For a sensitive gut, an isolate is worth the premium.

## Dose

One 25 g scoop delivers ~19 g protein (unflavored). Mixes cleanly into coffee, oats, or milk. In plain water it's thin, concentrate's lactose actually helps it feel less watery than isolate does, so unflavored Impact Whey in water is drinkable in a way unflavored isolate isn't.

## Brand context

Myprotein is a UK-founded brand (The Hut Group) and one of the few mass-market protein brands that runs genuinely competitive pricing in India *and* the UK without the middleman markup American whey brands add overseas. Impact Whey is their flagship SKU, it's the cheapest credible whey in most markets they serve.

## How I'm using it

Part of the morning rotation, not the whole of it. Rotates with Nutricost isolate depending on which tub is open. One scoop after a lift, sometimes a second in a midday oatmeal bowl.
