## Why isolate, not concentrate

Whey comes in three forms. **Concentrate** is ~70–80% protein with the fat and lactose left in — cheapest, but harder on a lactose-sensitive gut. **Isolate** is ~90%+ protein with almost all the fat and lactose filtered out. **Hydrolysate** is isolate broken into smaller peptides for faster absorption — and a much higher price tag.

Isolate is the middle ground: higher protein-per-scoop than concentrate, easier digestion, cleaner macros. For a 5 lb unflavored tub at the Nutricost price, it's the best value-per-gram of protein on Amazon US.

## What "unflavored" actually means

No sweetener, no cocoa, no vanillin — just whey. Mixes into anything without hijacking the taste: coffee, oats, a smoothie with actual fruit, a post-workout milk. The trade-off is it's not meant to be shaken up with plain water — flavorless whey in water tastes like diluted milk left out.

## Dose

One scoop (~30 g) delivers ~25–27 g of protein. Most evidence on hypertrophy and recovery points to 1.6–2.2 g protein per kg of bodyweight per day *total* — from food *plus* shakes. Whey is just a convenient way to close the gap when whole food doesn't.
