The Pro 1000 was the brush before the iO Series 5. Three years of two-minute sessions, twice a day, on a single round head with the soft pressure-feedback hum that Oral-B has been selling forever. It did the job it was sold for, and for the price (under $50 most of the year, often under $40 on a Target sale) it was the right starter electric.

## What worked

The 2-minute timer with the 30-second quadrant pulse. That alone made me brush longer and more evenly than any manual ever did. The single brush mode meant nothing to fiddle with: turn on, brush, turn off. The charging stand was small enough to live behind the soap dispenser without a fight.

## What didn't

No real pressure sensor. The brush would just keep grinding if you leaned on it, and three years in I noticed gum recession on my upper-left canine that I'm fairly sure was from over-pressing. The iO Series 5 has a pressure ring that fixed that habit in two weeks.

## When it retired

The battery degraded faster than the brush itself did. By year three, a single charge lasted six days instead of the fourteen I got out of it new. That, plus the upgrade urge, was the cue. The Pro 1000 still works; it lives in the travel kit now.
