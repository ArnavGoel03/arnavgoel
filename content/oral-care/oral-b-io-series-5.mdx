## Why I bought it

My old Oral-B Pro 1000 started losing charge after six days instead of two weeks and the bristles on every replacement head felt rougher than they used to. I wanted to upgrade without going all the way to the top of the iO line.

## What's different

Two things stand out versus the cheaper Oral-B brushes. The pressure sensor is a light ring around the neck that goes red if you press too hard, white if you're in the sweet spot — subtle but it fixed a habit I didn't know I had. And the motor is genuinely quieter: no more teeth-rattling whine, just a smooth hum.

## The 2-minute timer

Every 30 seconds it pulses to tell you to switch quadrants. Combined with the pressure ring, I actually finish a full two minutes now. With the old brush I'd drift off at 90 seconds and call it.

## The app

Don't bother. It maps your mouth into quadrants and tells you how long you spent in each. Interesting once, useless after. I keep Bluetooth off on the brush.

## Verdict

For $100 you're paying for the pressure sensor, the quieter motor, and the better head attachment mechanism. If your current brush works fine, skip it. If you're replacing a five-year-old Pro series, it's the easiest upgrade in my bathroom.
