Perfora's black manual toothbrush, charcoal-infused soft bristles on a slim recycled handle. The charcoal in the bristles is the marketing gimmick (it does not measurably whiten in a manual rotation), but the bristle quality is what earns it the rotation: tapered tips that actually reach the gumline without the rake-feel of cheap drugstore manuals.

I keep this in the bag for travel and in the bathroom for the days I want a manual instead of the electric. Perfora's DTC subscription drops a fresh head every three months, which is the right replacement cadence per ADA guidance and removes the "is this brush too old" decision from my hands.

A solid bridge between the cheap manual and a $30 brand-name manual. India-only DTC; if you live elsewhere, the closest equivalent is the Bite Charcoal Bristle line.
