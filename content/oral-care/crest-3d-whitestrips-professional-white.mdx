Crest's flagship at-home whitening kit at the time of buying — strips plus a battery-powered "light" the marketing says accelerates the result. Returned same-day. The peer-reviewed evidence on blue-light acceleration of hydrogen-peroxide whitening at this concentration is thin to non-existent; the strips do the work, the gadget is theatre.

For half the price, Crest's regular 3D Whitestrips Professional Effects deliver an indistinguishable result over the same number of days. The $50 here is a brand tax. If serious shade change is the goal, a dental-office hydrogen peroxide tray treatment is worth the step up; if not, the $25 strip pack ends the conversation.
